How to Play DailyDuel

Race to crack the daily five-letter word — and try to do it faster than your opponent.

The basic game

Every day, DailyDuel picks one secret five-letter word that is the same for every player. You have six guesses to find it. Type any real five-letter word and press Enter; each guess is checked against a dictionary of thousands of words, so real words are accepted and nonsense is rejected with a shake.

Reading the colors

Green — right letter, right spot. It belongs exactly there.
Yellow — right letter, wrong spot. The word has this letter elsewhere.
Grey — not in the word. Drop this letter for the rest of the round.

The on-screen keyboard mirrors these colors so you can track which letters are still live at a glance.

The duel twist

DailyDuel is more than a solo puzzle. In a duel you and an opponent solve the exact same word at the same time, side by side. You see your rival's board fill in with colors as they play — but never their letters, so there is no peeking at the answer. The first player to solve the word wins the crown. If both solve it, the faster time wins.

Ways to play

  • Practice — solve the daily word solo, no timer pressure, to sharpen your strategy.
  • Duel the Bot — race a smart computer opponent that uses real word-solving strategy and typically finishes in three to five guesses.
  • Duel a Friend, Live — create a room, share the link, and race a friend in real time on the same puzzle.
  • Friend Challenge — win today's puzzle, then send a link that carries your time for a friend to try to beat.

Ranks, XP, and streaks

Every game earns experience points, which raise your level and move you up the ranks from Bronze all the way to Crown. Winning on consecutive days builds a streak, and your daily record is set by your first completed game so you cannot farm or accidentally break it with replays. Wins, win rate, best solve time, and streaks are all tracked and stored privately in your browser.

A quick strategy tip

Open with a word rich in common letters to light up as many greens and yellows as possible, then use what you learn to narrow the field fast. In a duel, speed matters as much as accuracy — a confident early guess can be worth more than a perfect but slow one.
